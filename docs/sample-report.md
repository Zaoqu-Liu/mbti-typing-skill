# Sample Report

This sample shows the expected shape of a calibrated final report. It is intentionally compact; real reports can be longer when the evidence base is richer.

## Working Formulation

Current best-supported formulation: **ENTJ-like Te-Ni pattern with INTJ as the serious runner-up**.

Confidence: **medium**, not high. The evidence supports a directional formulation, but it is not enough to erase the INTJ and INFP alternatives.

## Why ENTJ Leads

The strongest evidence is not "organized" or "ambitious." Those are weak stereotypes.

The stronger pattern is:

- The person converts ambiguity into ownership, priorities, and next actions even without an external audience.
- In conflict, they move toward decision structure before emotional field-reading.
- Feedback about ineffectiveness triggers correction more than collapse.
- Meaning and values appear to sharpen action rather than replace action.

That pattern is more consistent with Te as an operating engine than with Te as a temporary stress shell.

## Why INTJ Remains Serious

INTJ remains plausible because:

- The person is highly sensitive to shallow reasoning.
- Depth and model quality seem identity-relevant.
- Some decisiveness may come after an internal convergence point rather than before it.

If future evidence shows that private synthesis consistently precedes external structuring, INTJ could overtake ENTJ.

## Why INFP Is Not Dismissed

INFP is weaker but not irrelevant.

Values intensity and meaning language can be genuine Fi evidence. The key question is whether values are the main decision engine or whether values operate inside a broader Te-Ni execution frame.

INFP would rise if:

- The person delays action until internal value alignment is complete.
- External structure appears mainly under stress.
- The person repeatedly chooses authenticity preservation over strategic leverage.

## Falsifiers

This formulation should change if new evidence shows:

- The person defaults to private model-building and only later externalizes structure.
- They organize mainly when anxious, threatened, or role-constrained.
- They routinely abandon effective paths because they violate a personal authenticity boundary.
- They read group emotion before defining goals, roles, or decisions.

## Practical Observation Prompts

For the next two weeks, track three moments:

1. When nobody expects output, what do you organize anyway?
2. When a plan is ambiguous, what irritates you first: wasted motion, wrong model, violated value, or relational tension?
3. After a conflict, do you recover by clarifying the system, refining the model, restoring value integrity, or repairing emotional connection?

The result becomes more reliable when repeated normal-state observations beat one dramatic anecdote.
