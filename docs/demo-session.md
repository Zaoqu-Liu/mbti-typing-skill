# Demo Session

This is a compact example of how a live session should feel. The point is not to prove the subject is a type from one exchange. The point is to show the loop: candidate set, targeted questions, evidence update, contradiction, next fork.

## User Prompt

```text
People say I am INFP because I care about meaning and I can be intense about values.
But I often test ENTJ, and under pressure I become very structured and impatient.
Do not give me a quick label. Keep the runner-up alive.
```

## Round 0: Intake

Current working candidates:

| Candidate | Why it stays alive | What could weaken it |
|---|---|---|
| ENTJ | Structured pressure response, impatience with drift, possible Te-Ni pattern | If structure is mostly anxiety management rather than default agency |
| INTJ | Strategic inward model-building could look like decisive Te from outside | If the person defaults to external coordination before private synthesis |
| INFP | Values intensity and meaning language could indicate Fi-Ne | If values are used as a compass after goals are already chosen |
| ENFJ | People may read intensity and direction as Fe-Ni leadership | If emotional attunement is not the default operating system |

Next fork: is the structure a default engine, a stress shell, or a borrowed role?

## Round 1 Questions

1. When there is no deadline and nobody is watching, do you naturally organize the environment, or do you first build a private model of what matters?
2. In conflict, is your first impulse to make the decision path explicit, protect a value boundary, or read the emotional field?
3. Give one example where your "meaning" concern made you more decisive, and one where it made you hesitate.
4. When a plan fails, do you feel more irritated by wasted motion, wrong assumptions, violated values, or emotional misalignment?
5. What kind of feedback hurts more: "you are ineffective", "you are shallow", "you are wrong", or "you are uncaring"?

## Example Evidence Update

Suppose the user answers:

```text
If nobody is watching, I still build systems. I hate vague goals.
But I do not organize people for harmony. I organize because unclear ownership wastes time.
The feedback that hurts most is "you are shallow"; "ineffective" makes me angry but also immediately corrective.
```

Ledger update:

| Observation | Supports | Contradicts | Alternative explanation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Builds systems without audience pressure | ENTJ, INTJ | weaker INFP | Role training or achievement culture |
| Dislikes unclear ownership more than emotional disharmony | ENTJ | weaker ENFJ | Current work environment may reward clarity |
| "Shallow" hurts more than "ineffective" | INTJ, INFP | weakens pure Te stereotype | Identity investment in depth |
| Ineffective feedback triggers correction | ENTJ | weakens INFP as leader | Could be learned coping |

Candidate update:

| Rank | Candidate | Status |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | ENTJ | Slight lead, but not locked |
| 2 | INTJ | Still serious because depth/private-model evidence is strong |
| 3 | INFP | Still possible if structure is compensatory |
| 4 | ENFJ | Weaker unless future evidence shows Fe-first regulation |

## Round 2: Targeted Duel

Current duel: ENTJ vs INTJ.

The next round should not ask "are you extroverted?" It should test Te-dom versus Ni-dom:

1. When you enter a messy project, do you first define ownership and measurable next actions, or privately isolate the underlying model error?
2. Which mistake do you forgive faster: a person who thinks deeply but does not move, or a person who moves quickly but misses the deeper pattern?
3. In your best state, do people experience you more as a force that organizes reality, or a person who sees where reality is going?

## Good Session Feel

A good session should make the user think:

```text
The system did not just ask generic MBTI questions.
It noticed the exact contradiction between values intensity and command-mode structure.
It did not throw away INTJ or INFP too early.
I can see what answer would change the result.
```
