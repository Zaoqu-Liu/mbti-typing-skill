## Model Caveat

This map uses the common Grant-style function-stack language because it is useful for qualitative interviewing. It is not the official scoring model of the MBTI instrument and should not be presented as a measured biological fact.

Use it as a hypothesis map:

- Dominant/auxiliary: likely default process.
- Tertiary: familiar but less mature support or release channel.
- Inferior: stress, shame, blind spot, or overcompensation risk.

## High-Risk Mistypes

### Turbulent NTJ as INFP

Why it happens:

- Rumination, identity crises, shame, tenderness, and sensitivity to criticism look like Feeling.
- Low social warmth can be mistaken for Introversion.
- Stress or burnout can make a normally executive person appear withdrawn and emotional.

Separate by:

- Naturalness of Te.
- Default idle cognition.
- Whether Fi is continuous compass or delayed protected channel.
- Whether values guide daily decisions or appear under threat, protection, art, animals, intimacy, or collapse.

### Role-Trained Introvert as Extravert

Why it happens:

- Leaders, doctors, teachers, founders, and managers may perform social command well.

Separate by:

- Energy after repeated people orchestration.
- What the person chooses when no role requires performance.
- Whether live interaction creates cognition or merely deploys prepared cognition.

### High-Conscientious P Type as J

Why it happens:

- Work discipline, academic pressure, or anxiety can create strong planning behavior.

Separate by:

- Need for closure vs ability to keep options psychologically alive.
- Whether plans are identity-comforting or simply useful scaffolds.

### Cultural Stoicism as Thinking Type

Why it happens:

- Gender norms, family background, or class mobility can suppress emotional language.

Separate by:

- Decision criterion under private, low-threat conditions.
- What the person protects when efficiency and values conflict.
- Whether emotional expression is absent, unsafe, unpracticed, or genuinely low-priority.

## Pair Discriminator Map

| Pair | Shared surface | Best discriminator |
|---|---|---|
| ENTJ vs INTJ | Strategic, Te/Ni, intense, future-focused | Idle default plus energy under command |
| ENTJ vs ESTJ | Te, leadership, blunt standards | Ni future architecture vs Si precedent |
| ENTJ vs ENFJ | Charismatic leadership | People as medium vs people as execution variable |
| INTJ vs INFJ | Ni, private intensity, future patterning | Te external systems vs Fe relational meaning |
| INTJ vs ISTJ | Reserved, structured, competent | Future model vs precedent continuity |
| INFP vs INFJ | Idealistic, reflective, emotionally deep | Fi personal congruence vs Ni-Fe meaning convergence |
| INFP vs ISFP | Fi, authenticity, private values | Ne possibility meaning vs Se embodied action |
| INFP vs ENFP | Fi-Ne values and possibilities | Solitary value processing vs external possibility generation |
| ENFP vs ENTP | Fast ideas, novelty, people | Fi meaning/value pull vs Ti model play |
| INTP vs ENTP | Ti/Ne concepts | Private precision vs live debate generation |
| INTP vs ISTP | Ti cool analysis | Abstract model vs live mechanical reality |
| ESTP vs ENTP | Quick, witty, opportunistic | Concrete tactical pressure vs conceptual possibility combat |
| ESFP vs ENFP | Expressive, novelty-seeking | Immediate reality vs meaning possibilities |
| ISFJ vs INFJ | Helpful, private, people-aware | Known obligations vs future pattern meaning |
| ESFJ vs ENFJ | Fe leadership and care | Established roles vs transformational vision |
| ESTJ vs ISTJ | Si/Te reliability | External command vs internal duty/continuity |
