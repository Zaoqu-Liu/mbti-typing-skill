## Usage Rules

- Ask 4-6 questions per round.
- Use concrete scenes. Avoid asking for type words directly.
- Prefer low-typing answer design: native selectable options when available, otherwise compact A/B/C/D/E choices.
- Use the final option as `Other / none of these - I will explain` unless the host automatically provides free-form input.
- Do not reveal option-to-type mappings before the answer.
- Prefer ranking top two options when the subject says several options fit.
- After every round, update the candidate set and write what each answer rules in and rules out.
- Do not reuse a question if its answer would only confirm the current favorite.
- For a known top-two battle, load `pair-duels.md` first and use this file only to fill missing probes.

## Core Function Probes

### Messy Problem Entry

Scene: "You inherit a chaotic project with unclear goals, messy people, and no reliable documentation. In the first hour, what do you do?"

- A. Define the outcome, owners, constraints, and first executable plan. `Te`
- B. Build a private model of what is really going on before moving people. `Ni/Ti`
- C. Talk to stakeholders to understand expectations and emotional alignment. `Fe`
- D. Inspect concrete artifacts, examples, and working parts before theorizing. `Se/Si`

Follow-up: "What feels irresponsible to skip?"

### Moral Ambiguity

Scene: "A close friend did something morally questionable but says it was for someone they love. What happens first?"

- A. I internally test whether the act violates my values. `Fi`
- B. I ask for concrete facts and consequences before judging. `Te/Ti`
- C. I map who is hurt, who is responsible, and how to repair the relationship field. `Fe`
- D. I compare it to known cases and precedents. `Si`

Follow-up: "Do you feel pulled into the moral emotion before facts are clear?"

### New Domain Learning

Scene: "You must enter a field you know almost nothing about."

- A. Start doing small tasks and learn from feedback. `Se/Te`
- B. Search for the hidden structure and wait until the map clicks. `Ni`
- C. Generate many analogies and possible connections. `Ne`
- D. Build a careful foundation from reliable references. `Si/Ti`

Follow-up: "What makes you trust that you understand it?"

### Decision Under Ambiguity

Scene: "A major life decision has incomplete data."

- A. A direction appears first; I then gather evidence to test or execute it. `Ni-Te`
- B. I compare past cases, risks, and proven paths. `Si-Te`
- C. I list options and keep possibilities open until the last responsible moment. `Ne`
- D. I ask which choice I can personally stand behind, even if inefficient. `Fi`

Follow-up: "When people disagree, what actually changes your mind?"

### Conflict Repair

Scene: "You snapped at someone important. They are hurt."

- A. I explain the logic, solve the cause, then repair emotion after I cool down. `Te with delayed Fi`
- B. I immediately track their emotional state and repair the bond first. `Fe`
- C. I withdraw to understand what I really feel and whether a boundary was crossed. `Fi`
- D. I analyze the exact trigger and principle that failed. `Ti/Ni`

Follow-up: "What is your first repair language: apology, explanation, solution, reassurance, or space?"

## Adjacent-Type Discriminators

### ENTJ vs INTJ

Both can be strategic, intense, future-oriented, and Te/Ni-heavy. Do not separate them with generic ambition or intelligence.

High-value questions:

1. **Idle default:** "On a free morning with no obligations, where does your mind drift?"
   - ENTJ-leaning: execution order, people allocation, leverage, external optimization.
   - INTJ-leaning: private models, future architecture, meaning, conceptual refinement.

2. **Authority energy:** "When you have authority over a room and must coordinate many capable people, what happens to your energy?"
   - ENTJ-leaning: activated, sharper, enjoys command if the mission matters.
   - INTJ-leaning: can perform it, but sustained people orchestration is usually costly.

3. **Long isolation:** "After several days with no meaningful human contact, what changes?"
   - ENTJ-leaning: rest first, then agitation or under-stimulation.
   - INTJ-leaning: may feel restored longer if projects and mental material are available.

4. **Idea validation:** "When you discuss a new idea, are you creating it with people or stress-testing an already formed model?"
   - ENTJ-leaning: external pressure and dialogue speed up formation or execution.
   - INTJ-leaning: dialogue tests, translates, or operationalizes a mostly internal model.

5. **Stress signature:** "Under heavy pressure, do you lose emotional value contact, body regulation, strategic patience, or social tolerance first?"
   - ENTJ: watch inferior-Fi collapse, shame, value confusion, harshness, or delayed vulnerability.
   - INTJ: watch inferior-Se overload, sensory impulsivity, body dysregulation, or present-moment flooding.

Close only after testing default cognition, energy metabolism, and stress signature.

### INFP vs INFJ

Both can look reflective, idealistic, private, and emotionally intense.

- Values source:
  - INFP: personal congruence, inner rightness, authenticity.
  - INFJ: relational meaning, future implications, hidden motives, collective impact.
- Idea pattern:
  - INFP: Fi-Ne exploration of possibilities around values.
  - INFJ: Ni-Fe convergence into one interpretive pattern.
- Conflict:
  - INFP: "This violates who I am or what I can endorse."
  - INFJ: "This changes what this relationship or situation means."

Question: "When a friend makes a hurtful choice, do you first ask whether you can morally endorse it, or what it reveals about the relationship and future pattern?"

### INFP vs ENTJ/INTJ

False positives happen when a Te/Ni person has rumination, identity crises, tenderness, or high neuroticism.

Separate by:

- Default daily process: personal values and emotional congruence vs goals, systems, models, execution.
- Relationship to Te: exhausting emergency tool vs natural operating system.
- Relationship to Fi: constant inner compass vs delayed, protected, role-dependent, or crisis-activated channel.
- Stress: Te grip in INFP vs Fi or Se-related breakdown in Te/Ni types.

Question: "When nobody is demanding anything, do you naturally check inner value alignment, optimize external systems, or refine a future model?"

### ESTJ vs ENTJ

Both can be Te-forward, blunt, efficient, and managerial.

- ESTJ: precedent, reliability, known rules, continuity, operational discipline.
- ENTJ: future-state architecture, strategic leap, reconfiguration, leverage.

Question: "If an old but profitable system works, do you first stabilize and standardize it, or ask whether it blocks the future architecture?"

### ENFJ vs ENTJ

Both can lead rooms and influence people.

- ENFJ: people alignment is the medium of action.
- ENTJ: people alignment is a constraint or lever for execution.

Question: "When a team is emotionally misaligned but the plan is correct, do you experience the emotional field as the main problem or as one execution risk among others?"

### ENTP vs INTP

Both can be idea-rich and analytical.

- ENTP: external debate generates energy and new possibilities.
- INTP: internal precision and conceptual independence dominate; debate tests rather than fuels.

Question: "Does live argument make your thinking better in real time, or mainly expose what your private model still needs to clean up?"

### ISFP vs INFP

Both can be Fi-led.

- ISFP: values meet concrete action, aesthetics, body, immediate reality.
- INFP: values meet meanings, possibilities, stories, and alternate worlds.

Question: "When moved by something, do you want to act or make something tangible now, or explore what it means and what else it could become?"

## Stress And Recovery Probes

Use stress data carefully. A stress behavior can be type, trauma, lifestyle, health, or environment.

Questions:

1. "When overloaded, what do you do that feels unlike your normal self?"
2. "What restores you fastest: solitude, people, body sensation, order, novelty, emotional expression, or closure?"
3. "What do you hide because it would make you feel weak?"
4. "After public criticism, what loops for 48 hours: facts, shame, revenge, relationship, meaning, or bodily agitation?"
5. "When very tired, do you need emotional processing or just low-cognition sensory recovery?"

Interpretation:

- Inferior-function patterns are useful only when repeated and costly.
- Tertiary-function pressure release is not the same as inferior grip.
- Body-based recovery does not automatically mean Se dominance.

## Big Five Cross-Checks

Ask after MBTI hypotheses form, not before.

- Openness: "Do you seek novelty for its own sake, for transfer into a system, for beauty, or for possibility?"
- Conscientiousness: "Can you sustain boring execution after the initial vision fades?"
- Extraversion: "Which energizes you: social warmth, dominance, live stimulation, recognition, or none?"
- Agreeableness: "Do you avoid conflict because harmony matters, or tolerate conflict if the objective requires it?"
- Neuroticism: "What triggers disproportionate rumination: rejection, failure, uncertainty, loss of control, moral guilt, or bodily threat?"

Use mismatches as prompts:

- High dominance but low social warmth can mimic Extraversion in MBTI without high Big Five Extraversion.
- High neuroticism can make many types look introverted, feeling, or turbulent.
- High conscientiousness can make perceiving types look judging in work contexts.

## Contradiction Follow-Ups

Use these when answers conflict:

- "Give two real examples, one where this pattern happened and one where it did not."
- "What part is instinctive, and what part is learned from role, culture, or work?"
- "What does this behavior cost you afterward?"
- "If I removed the audience, deadline, and status stakes, would the behavior remain?"
- "What would your spouse, teammate, or close friend say is missing from your self-description?"
- "What interpretation have I just made that feels wrong or overdramatic?"

## Round Templates

### Round 1: Claim Triage

- One moral/process question.
- One new-domain learning question.
- One social-energy question.
- One stress/recovery question.

Goal: reduce obvious false positives; do not conclude.

### Round 2: Contradiction Drill

- Ask only about contradictions from Round 1.
- Include "what exactly is in your head?" follow-ups.

Goal: distinguish surface behavior from mechanism.

### Round 3: Top-Two Duel

- Ask 4 direct discriminators for the top pair.
- Include one counterexample request.

Goal: force runner-up comparison.

### Round 4: Cross-Framework Check

- Big Five sketch.
- Role/culture/attachment alternative explanations.
- External observer check if available.

Goal: prevent MBTI from explaining everything.

### Round 5: Final Falsifier

- Ask: "What evidence would make this conclusion wrong?"
- Ask the subject to reject any inaccurate framing.

Goal: avoid beautiful but false closure.
