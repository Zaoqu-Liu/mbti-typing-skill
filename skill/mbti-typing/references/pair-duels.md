## How To Use

Use this file when two candidate types remain close. Ask 2-4 questions from the relevant pair. Do not ask all questions. Pick the ones that attack the actual uncertainty.

For every duel, record:

- Shared surface.
- Strongest discriminator.
- Evidence that would make each side lose.
- Whether the answer is normal-state or stress-state.

## NT And NF Duels

### ENTJ vs INTJ

Shared surface: strategic, future-focused, Te/Ni, high standards.

Killer questions:

1. "When free and alone, does your mind naturally optimize external systems or refine a private future model?"
2. "Does commanding capable people make you sharper, or is it a necessary cost of implementation?"
3. "In conflict, do you externalize logic immediately or withdraw until the model is clean?"

ENTJ loses if: leadership energy is mostly costly, external organization is role-only, private modeling dominates idle time.

INTJ loses if: live command reliably energizes cognition, idle time becomes people/resource optimization, decisions externalize before private model completion.

### ENTJ vs ESTJ

Shared surface: Te, command, standards, decisiveness.

Killer questions:

1. "Do you trust proven operating rules until they fail, or replace them when they block the future architecture?"
2. "When data is incomplete, do you compare with precedent or jump to a strategic model?"
3. "What irritates you more: inconsistency with standards or lack of future leverage?"

ENTJ loses if: precedent and continuity are the core trust source.

ESTJ loses if: stable legacy systems are intolerable when they conflict with a future architecture.

### ENTP vs ENFP

Shared surface: novelty, speed, talk, possibility.

Killer questions:

1. "When exploring ideas, are you protecting personal meaning or stress-testing an impersonal model?"
2. "In debate, do you feel guilty for violating values, or excited by sharper logic?"
3. "Which betrayal hurts more: someone attacks your values, or someone refuses to think clearly?"

ENTP loses if: value congruence repeatedly outranks model elegance.

ENFP loses if: personal meaning is secondary to conceptual play and adversarial testing.

### INTP vs ENTP

Shared surface: Ti/Ne, concepts, argument, independence.

Killer questions:

1. "Does live debate generate your best thinking or mostly expose what your private model needs?"
2. "After a stimulating argument, are you charged to keep engaging or eager to retreat and refine?"
3. "Do people primarily experience you as outwardly provocative or privately exacting?"

INTP loses if: external argument is the default engine of thought.

ENTP loses if: live debate is optional and private precision is the true center.

### INTJ vs INFJ

Shared surface: Ni, privacy, intensity, future patterns.

Killer questions:

1. "When your prediction is ignored, is the offense mainly inefficiency/stupidity or relational/moral consequence?"
2. "Do you naturally move from insight to systems, or from insight to people/meaning alignment?"
3. "Under stress, do you become more bodily impulsive or more socially overwhelmed by others' needs?"

INTJ loses if: relational meaning and interpersonal alignment are the default axis.

INFJ loses if: external systems and impersonal execution dominate after insight.

### INFP vs INFJ

Shared surface: depth, sensitivity, ideals, private intensity.

Killer questions:

1. "Is your first compass inner congruence or the hidden pattern in people and relationships?"
2. "Do possibilities branch around what feels personally true, or converge into one likely meaning?"
3. "In conflict, do you feel 'I cannot endorse this' or 'I see what this relationship is becoming'?"

INFP loses if: convergence and relational patterning dominate personal congruence.

INFJ loses if: personal value authenticity remains central even when social meaning points elsewhere.

### INFP vs ENFP

Shared surface: Fi-Ne, values, imagination, identity.

Killer questions:

1. "Does contact with people generate possibilities, or do people interrupt private value processing?"
2. "When excited by an idea, do you first tell others or privately test whether it is yours?"
3. "After social inspiration, do you expand outward or need to return inward to locate yourself?"

INFP loses if: external possibility exchange is the default generator.

ENFP loses if: private congruence checking precedes outward exploration.

### ENFJ vs ENTJ

Shared surface: leadership, vision, influence.

Killer questions:

1. "Are people the medium of the work, or the variable that must align for execution?"
2. "What feels more urgent: emotional buy-in or objective leverage?"
3. "When the team is hurt but the plan is right, what do you repair first?"

ENFJ loses if: people alignment is consistently subordinate to execution.

ENTJ loses if: emotional field management is the natural first lever, not a cost.

## SJ And SP Duels

### ISTJ vs ISFJ

Shared surface: duty, memory, reliability, continuity.

Killer questions:

1. "When a responsibility is unclear, do you first define the standard or protect the person affected?"
2. "Is your memory organized around facts/procedures or people/obligations?"
3. "What bothers you more: unreliable execution or social neglect?"

ISTJ loses if: social obligation organizes the memory and action system.

ISFJ loses if: impersonal standards and procedure integrity dominate care.

### ESTJ vs ESFJ

Shared surface: order, community standards, responsibility.

Killer questions:

1. "Do you enforce norms because they make systems work or because they preserve the social fabric?"
2. "When someone fails, do you first correct performance or restore belonging?"
3. "Which is harder to tolerate: incompetence or visible disregard for people?"

ESTJ loses if: harmony and belonging are the core organizing principle.

ESFJ loses if: performance standards override relational repair by default.

### ISFP vs ISTP

Shared surface: quiet, concrete, independent, action-oriented.

Killer questions:

1. "When acting, are you protecting personal value/aesthetic truth or solving the mechanics?"
2. "Do you trust what feels right or what works cleanly?"
3. "When criticized, does it hit identity/taste or competence/logic?"

ISFP loses if: mechanical precision is the core satisfaction.

ISTP loses if: private value expression drives action even when less efficient.

### ESTP vs ESFP

Shared surface: Se, action, social presence, immediacy.

Killer questions:

1. "In the moment, are you optimizing the tactic or expressing the experience?"
2. "Do you read people to maneuver effectively or to connect/energize authentically?"
3. "What feels more alive: winning the live problem or fully inhabiting the moment?"

ESTP loses if: personal expression and emotional presence dominate tactical control.

ESFP loses if: tactical advantage and impersonal leverage dominate expression.

### ISTP vs ESTP

Shared surface: Ti-Se, practical troubleshooting, calm under pressure.

Killer questions:

1. "Does pressure from people sharpen you or annoy you?"
2. "Do you seek the live arena or prefer the system/problem without social heat?"
3. "After action, do you want more stimulation or private decompression?"

ISTP loses if: public live pressure reliably generates energy.

ESTP loses if: social arena is optional and private troubleshooting is preferred.

### ISFP vs ESFP

Shared surface: Fi-Se, aesthetics, personal expression, direct experience.

Killer questions:

1. "Do you express values privately through selected action, or publicly through presence?"
2. "Does attention energize expression or distort it?"
3. "When moved, do you first protect the feeling or share the experience?"

ISFP loses if: public experiential expression is naturally energizing.

ESFP loses if: visibility often contaminates private authenticity.

## Cross-Temperament Mistypes

### INFP vs INTP

Shared surface: private, abstract, outsider, internally driven.

Killer questions:

1. "When something is wrong, is it wrong because it violates inner values or because the model is incoherent?"
2. "Do you defend your identity or your logical precision first?"
3. "What is more painful: being morally misunderstood or intellectually misrepresented?"

INFP loses if: logical coherence is protected even against personal values.

INTP loses if: personal congruence repeatedly outranks analytic elegance.

### INFJ vs INTJ

Use the NT/NF duel above. Add: "Do you naturally translate insight into care language or execution architecture?"

### ENFP vs ESFP

Shared surface: energetic, expressive, novelty-seeking.

Killer questions:

1. "Does novelty become meanings and possibilities, or stay embodied and immediate?"
2. "Do you chase the idea behind the experience or the experience itself?"
3. "When bored, do you need conceptual possibility or sensory/social stimulation?"

ENFP loses if: experience itself is the center.

ESFP loses if: the experience mainly serves possibility and meaning.

### ENTP vs ESTP

Shared surface: quick, bold, improvisational, provocative.

Killer questions:

1. "Do you improvise with concepts or with live reality?"
2. "Would you rather win the argument or win the room/problem?"
3. "When constraints appear, do they become tactical terrain or conceptual material?"

ENTP loses if: live tactical adaptation dominates conceptual play.

ESTP loses if: abstract possibility is more energizing than real-time action.

### INFJ vs ISFJ

Shared surface: private care, responsibility, people-awareness.

Killer questions:

1. "Do you trust what has consistently mattered, or what the pattern predicts will matter?"
2. "Is care expressed through known obligations or future developmental meaning?"
3. "When someone changes, do you compare to their history or see a new trajectory?"

INFJ loses if: concrete continuity and remembered obligations dominate.

ISFJ loses if: future-pattern interpretation overrides precedent.

### INTJ vs INTP

Shared surface: private, analytical, independent, abstract.

Killer questions:

1. "Do you want a working strategic conclusion or a clean model?"
2. "Does closure help you move, or does it feel like premature simplification?"
3. "When the model is imperfect but usable, do you execute or keep refining?"

INTJ loses if: precision outranks strategic closure by default.

INTP loses if: imperfect strategic closure is acceptable once it moves the future.
